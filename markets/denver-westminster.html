Why Westminster?

Westminster sits north of Denver along the US-36 corridor, perfectly positioned between downtown Denver and Boulder. The city has invested heavily in creating a new downtown district around the former Westminster Mall site, bringing mixed-use development, parks, and a growing roster of restaurants and shops. The Butterfly Pavilion, a unique invertebrate zoo and nature center, is a family favorite, and Standley Lake offers boating and fishing with Rocky Mountain views as the backdrop.

The US-36 Bus Rapid Transit Flatiron Flyer system connects Westminster to both downtown Denver and Boulder with express service, and the RTD B Line commuter rail provides another transit option from the Westminster Station. Ball Corporation, Trimble, and Maxar Technologies are among the major employers in the area. Westminster Public Schools and Adams 12 Five Star Schools both serve the community, with several high-performing schools in the district.

Average one-bedroom rents in Westminster hover around $1,450, with newer communities near the new downtown district listing in the $1,600 to $1,900 range. Older communities along Federal Boulevard and Sheridan Boulevard offer more budget-friendly options in the $1,100 to $1,300 range.
