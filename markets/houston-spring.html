Why Spring?

Spring is a sprawling unincorporated community north of Houston that benefits from proximity to The Woodlands' amenities and employment centers without The Woodlands' price premium. Old Town Spring, a historic shopping district with Victorian-era buildings, locally owned shops, and seasonal festivals, gives the area a distinctive character. The ExxonMobil campus and the broader I-45 north corridor are a short commute from most Spring neighborhoods, making it a popular choice for energy sector workers who want more space for their dollar.

Spring's location along both I-45 and the Hardy Toll Road provides dual-corridor access to downtown Houston, while the Grand Parkway connects to suburban employment centers in every direction. The Klein and Spring ISD schools serve the area with generally solid performance. At an average one-bedroom rent of $1,200 per month, Spring is notably more affordable than neighboring The Woodlands while sharing access to much of the same retail, dining, and entertainment infrastructure.

Spring's expansive geography means there is significant pricing variation between different parts of the community.
