Why Lakewood?

Lakewood is Denver's western neighbor and the gateway to the foothills. With a population of over 157,000, it is the fifth-largest city in Colorado and offers a lifestyle that splits the difference between urban Denver and mountain-town living. Red Rocks Amphitheatre, one of the most iconic concert venues in the world, sits at Lakewood's western edge, and the city's higher-elevation neighborhoods offer unobstructed views of the Front Range. Bear Creek Lake Park provides fishing, paddleboarding, and trail running without leaving city limits, and the Colorado Mills mall anchors the retail scene along the I-70 corridor.

The RTD W Line light rail connects Lakewood to downtown Denver's Union Station in about 25 minutes, with stops at the Federal Center, a major government employment campus employing over 6,000 federal workers. Jeffco Public Schools serves the city and is one of the largest school districts in Colorado. Belmar, a mixed-use development in the heart of Lakewood, offers restaurants, shops, a movie theater, and apartment living in a walkable town center format.

Average one-bedroom rents in Lakewood are around $1,450, with newer communities near Belmar and along the light rail corridor listing in the $1,600 to $1,900 range. Older communities along Colfax Avenue and Alameda offer more affordable options in the $1,100 to $1,300 range.
