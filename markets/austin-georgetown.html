Why Georgetown?

Georgetown has been one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States, more than doubling its population in the past decade. The city's crown jewel is its historic downtown square, widely regarded as the most beautiful town square in Texas, lined with Victorian-era buildings housing locally owned restaurants, antique shops, and galleries. Southwestern University, one of the oldest universities in Texas, adds an academic presence, and Blue Hole Park offers a stunning natural swimming hole that draws visitors from across the region.

Georgetown ISD schools are well-regarded, and the city's Sun City community is one of the largest active adult retirement communities in the country. But Georgetown is not just for retirees. Young families and professionals are increasingly choosing Georgetown for its small-town feel, lower cost of living compared to central Austin, and improving I-35 connectivity that makes the commute to Austin's tech corridor viable. At an average one-bedroom rent of $1,300 per month, Georgetown offers genuine value relative to its quality of life.

The city's rapid growth has brought new apartment communities to the I-35 corridor and the Wolf Ranch area, and these developments often offer competitive lease-up pricing. Our AI monitors these opportunities to find where your budget goes furthest in Georgetown's evolving rental market.
