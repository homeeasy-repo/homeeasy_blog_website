Why Aurora?

Aurora is the third-largest city in Colorado and one of the most culturally diverse communities in the Rocky Mountain region. Stretching from Denver's eastern border all the way to the plains, Aurora offers everything from urban neighborhoods near the Anschutz Medical Campus to suburban communities near Cherry Creek State Park. The University of Colorado Anschutz Medical Campus is the largest employer, with the Children's Hospital Colorado, UCHealth University of Colorado Hospital, and the Veterans Affairs Medical Center all located on the sprawling campus. Buckley Space Force Base adds another major employment anchor on the city's eastern side.

Aurora's diversity is reflected in its restaurant scene, with authentic Ethiopian, Korean, Vietnamese, and Mexican restaurants concentrated along Havana Street and Colfax Avenue. The RTD R Line light rail connects Aurora to downtown Denver, Denver Tech Center, and Denver International Airport, and the city's I-225 corridor provides direct freeway access. Cherry Creek State Park and the Aurora Reservoir offer outdoor recreation without driving to the mountains, and the Stanley Marketplace in the Stapleton-adjacent area has become a popular food hall and community gathering spot.

Average one-bedroom rents in Aurora are around $1,400, which is meaningfully lower than Denver proper. Communities near the Anschutz campus list in the $1,500 to $1,800 range, while neighborhoods in central Aurora along Alameda and Mississippi avenues offer options below $1,200.
