Why Boulder?

Boulder is unlike any other city in the Denver metro. Nestled against the Flatirons at the mouth of Boulder Canyon, it combines a major research university, a thriving tech and natural products industry, and some of the best outdoor recreation access in the country. The Pearl Street Mall is the heart of downtown, a pedestrian-only stretch lined with restaurants, shops, and street performers that buzzes year-round. The University of Colorado Boulder enrolls over 35,000 students and employs thousands of faculty and staff, and its research programs have spawned a local ecosystem of startups and federal research labs including NIST, NOAA, and NCAR.

The outdoor lifestyle is not just marketing in Boulder. The city maintains over 46,000 acres of open space and 155 miles of trails, and the Chautauqua Park trailhead puts you at the base of the Flatirons in minutes. Boulder Creek Path runs through the center of town, and Boulder Reservoir offers swimming and paddleboarding in summer. Major employers beyond CU include Google, Ibotta, and a concentration of natural and organic food companies. The Flatiron Flyer bus rapid transit connects Boulder to Denver in about 45 minutes via US-36.

Boulder's desirability comes with a premium. Average one-bedroom rents are around $1,700, with downtown and Pearl Street-adjacent units listing in the $1,900 to $2,500 range. Apartments in north Boulder near the Diagonal Highway and in the Table Mesa area south of campus tend to be more affordable.
