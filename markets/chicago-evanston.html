Why Evanston?

Evanston occupies a unique position in the Chicago metro as a suburb that functions like a small city. Home to Northwestern University and its 22,000-plus students and faculty, Evanston has a walkable downtown centered on Davis Street and Chicago Avenue with independent restaurants, bookstores, coffee shops, and a thriving arts scene. The Lake Michigan shoreline runs along the city's eastern edge with public beaches at Clark Street, Lighthouse Landing, and Lee Street that fill up on summer weekends. The CTA Purple Line connects downtown Evanston to Chicago's Loop in about 35 minutes, making it practical to live in Evanston and commute into the city without a car.

Evanston is one of Chicago's most diverse inner suburbs, with a mix of students, academics, young professionals, and families. Evanston Township High School is one of the most recognized public high schools in Illinois, and the city's elementary schools are well-regarded. Major employers beyond Northwestern include NorthShore University HealthSystem, Rotary International's headquarters, and a growing roster of tech startups. The city also borders the Rogers Park and Edgewater neighborhoods of Chicago, giving residents easy access to both suburban amenities and urban energy.

Average one-bedroom rents in Evanston are around $1,500, with lakefront and downtown-adjacent units listing in the $1,700 to $2,200 range. Apartments west of Ridge Avenue and near the Main Street CTA stop tend to be more affordable.
