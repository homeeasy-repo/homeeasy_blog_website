Why Katy?

Katy has earned its reputation as one of Houston's most desirable suburbs thanks to the nationally recognized Katy Independent School District, which consistently ranks among the top districts in Texas. Families relocating to the Houston area routinely target Katy first, and that demand has fueled a wave of new apartment construction along the Grand Parkway and I-10 corridor. The broader Katy area, including master-planned communities like Cinco Ranch and Cross Creek Ranch, offers a population well over 300,000 in the extended zip codes, giving renters access to every retail and dining option imaginable without driving into the city.

The Energy Corridor, home to BP, ConocoPhillips, and dozens of oil and gas firms, sits just east of Katy along I-10, putting thousands of high-paying energy sector jobs within a 15 to 20 minute commute. Katy Mills Mall anchors the local retail scene, and the city's parks and recreation programs are among the best-funded in the Houston metro. Average one-bedroom rents hover around $1,350, with newer luxury communities along the Grand Parkway listing in the $1,500 to $1,800 range. Older communities closer to Old Town Katy can dip below $1,200 for updated units.

New apartment communities in Katy frequently offer two to three months of free rent on 14-month leases to attract tenants ahead of full occupancy.
