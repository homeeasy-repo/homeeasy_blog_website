Why Friendswood?

Friendswood is one of the Houston metro's best-kept secrets for renters who want a small-town atmosphere without sacrificing access to the city. Straddling the border of Harris and Galveston counties, this community of about 43,000 residents is known for its tree-lined streets, strong Friendswood ISD schools, and a downtown area that hosts farmers markets and community events throughout the year. It sits between the Texas Medical Center to the north and Galveston Island to the south, making it an ideal midpoint for professionals who work in either direction.

NASA's Johnson Space Center and the surrounding Clear Lake aerospace corridor are about 10 minutes east, and the I-45 corridor provides access to downtown Houston in approximately 30 minutes. Friendswood's Centennial Park and Stevenson Park offer walking trails and sports facilities, and the city's low crime rate consistently ranks it among the safest communities in the Houston area. The limited apartment inventory compared to larger suburbs means less turnover and a quieter living environment, though it also means the best units go quickly.

Average one-bedroom rents in Friendswood sit around $1,350.
