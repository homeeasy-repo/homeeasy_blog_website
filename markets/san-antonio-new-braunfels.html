Why New Braunfels?

New Braunfels is one of the fastest-growing cities in Texas and one of the most charming. Founded by German settlers in 1845, the city retains its heritage through annual Wurstfest celebrations, the Gruene Historic District (home to the oldest dance hall in Texas, Gruene Hall), and a downtown lined with German bakeries and beer gardens. The Comal River, the shortest navigable river in the United States, runs through the heart of town and is the most popular tubing destination in Texas. The Guadalupe River adds another option for kayaking and rafting, and Schlitterbahn Waterpark draws visitors from across the state.

New Braunfels sits on IH-35 roughly midway between San Antonio and Austin, making it a viable commuter location for workers in either city. The drive to downtown San Antonio is about 30 minutes, and Austin is approximately 45 minutes north. New Braunfels ISD and Comal ISD both serve the community with strong academic programs, and the city's population has surged past 98,000 as families discover the combination of Hill Country living, affordable housing, and good schools. The Creekside and Vintage Oaks master-planned communities have attracted new residents, and retail development along IH-35 has expanded rapidly.

Average one-bedroom rents in New Braunfels are around $1,250, making it one of the most affordable options along the I-35 corridor. Newer communities near the Creekside development list in the $1,400 to $1,600 range.
