Why Addison?

Addison packs more restaurants per capita than almost anywhere in America. With over 180 dining options in just 4.4 square miles, this compact town between Dallas and Plano has built its identity around food, festivals, and walkability. Addison Kaboom Town, Oktoberfest, and Taste Addison draw tens of thousands of visitors annually, and living here means those experiences are a walk from your front door rather than a drive.

Despite its small geographic footprint, Addison is home to a significant number of corporate offices and has more hotel rooms than residents. The Addison Airport serves private aviation, and the town's central location along the Dallas North Tollway provides quick access to jobs in both Uptown Dallas and the Plano corporate corridor. At an average one-bedroom rent of $1,400 per month, Addison offers a uniquely urban lifestyle within a North Texas suburb, with more walkable dining and entertainment than most cities ten times its size.

Addison's apartment communities are well-maintained and compete aggressively for residents given the town's small geography. Seasonal promotions, especially during summer move-in season, can yield significant concessions.
