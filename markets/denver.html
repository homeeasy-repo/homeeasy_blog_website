Why Denver?

We track 865 buildings across the Denver metro. 694 of them are in our portfolio. That means we know their pricing, their availability, their concession schedules, and their approval policies -- not from a listing site, from the source.

The Denver rental market is driven by supply waves. Over 8,000 new units are delivering in 2026, mostly in the luxury segment. When new supply hits, existing buildings respond with concessions: free months, reduced deposits, waived fees. The data shows where these pricing gaps open before listing sites catch up. Capitol Hill has 118 buildings starting around $1,813. RiNo starts at $1,115. Cherry Creek runs $2,075+ median. The spread between neighborhoods is wide -- your dollar goes very different distances depending on where you look.

Colorado's statewide source-of-income protection law means the majority of Denver buildings accept housing vouchers -- a significantly higher rate than Texas metros. We have verified voucher acceptance, showing procedures, and leasing contacts across the buildings we work with. Whether you need voucher-friendly buildings, flexible credit policies, or self-guided tours, we have the data before you waste an application fee.
