Why Sugar Land?

Sugar Land is one of Houston's most polished suburbs, combining upscale retail, a nationally recognized school district, and one of the most culturally diverse populations in the metro area. Sugar Land Town Square serves as the city's downtown, with restaurants, a Marriott hotel, an ice rink, and a plaza that hosts concerts and festivals year-round. The Smart Financial Centre draws national touring acts, and the Fort Bend Children's Discovery Center gives families another reason to stay close to home instead of driving into the city.

Fort Bend ISD is one of the most ethnically diverse school districts in the country and consistently earns high academic ratings. Major employers in the area include Schlumberger, Fluor Corporation, and Nalco Champion, all headquartered or with major offices in the Sugar Land and Missouri City corridor along US-59. The commute to the Texas Medical Center runs about 30 minutes outside of rush hour, making Sugar Land popular with healthcare professionals who want suburban living without an extreme drive.

Average one-bedroom rents in Sugar Land sit around $1,450, with luxury communities near Town Square and along University Boulevard pushing closer to $1,700. Older communities along US-90A offer more affordable options in the $1,100 to $1,300 range.
