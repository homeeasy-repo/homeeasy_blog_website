Why Round Rock?

Round Rock is Austin's largest suburb and a major tech employment center in its own right. Dell Technologies maintains its global headquarters here, and the company's campus along SH-45 employs thousands of workers. Emerson Automation Solutions, Kalahari Resorts, and a growing list of tech firms have also chosen Round Rock, giving residents the option of working locally without commuting into Austin. The Round Rock Premium Outlets and IKEA draw shoppers from across Central Texas, and the Dell Diamond hosts the Round Rock Express, the city's Triple-A baseball team.

Round Rock ISD is one of the highest-rated school districts in the Austin metro, with multiple high schools earning national recognition. The Old Settlers Park recreation complex covers over 600 acres with sports fields, a lake, and event space, and Brushy Creek trails wind through the northern part of the city. The IH-35 corridor connects Round Rock to downtown Austin in about 25 minutes outside of rush hour, and the SH-130 toll road offers a faster bypass route. Average one-bedroom rents sit around $1,400, which is notably lower than comparable apartments in central Austin, while two-bedrooms average around $1,700.

New apartment construction continues along the University Boulevard and IH-35 corridors, and these communities frequently offer move-in specials to attract tenants.
