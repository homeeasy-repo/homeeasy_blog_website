Why The Woodlands?

The Woodlands is the gold standard of master-planned communities in Texas. Developed by George Mitchell in the 1970s, it has grown into a self-contained city of over 120,000 residents with its own corporate district, performing arts center, and one of the best trail systems in the state. The Woodlands Waterway runs through the heart of Town Center, lined with restaurants, shops, and luxury apartment communities that offer a walkable urban experience inside a heavily wooded suburban setting.

ExxonMobil's sprawling campus at the southern edge of The Woodlands employs over 10,000 people, and Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Huntsman Corporation, and Anadarko Petroleum (now Occidental) all have their headquarters here. The Conroe ISD and Tomball ISD schools serving The Woodlands consistently rank among the top in the Houston metro. The Cynthia Woods Mitchell Pavilion hosts major concerts, and Market Street offers upscale shopping and dining in a town square setting.

Average one-bedroom rents in The Woodlands hover around $1,500, with luxury Waterway-adjacent communities listing in the $1,800 to $2,200 range. Communities in the northern villages near Creekside Park offer slightly lower pricing.
