Why Boerne?

Boerne (pronounced "Bernie") is the Hill Country gem northwest of San Antonio that has grown from a quiet small town into one of the most desirable suburbs in the metro area. The historic Main Street, Hauptstrasse, is lined with boutique shops, wine tasting rooms, antique stores, and locally owned restaurants that give the town a character distinctly different from typical suburban sprawl. The Cave Without a Name, one of the most beautiful show caves in Texas, sits just outside of town, and Cibolo Creek runs through the Cibolo Nature Center, offering hiking trails and wildlife viewing within walking distance of downtown.

Boerne ISD is one of the highest-rated school districts in the San Antonio metro, and the district's reputation is a primary driver of the city's growth. IH-10 connects Boerne to downtown San Antonio in about 30 minutes, and the expanding medical and corporate corridors along the I-10 west side have shortened commutes for many residents. The broader Boerne area, including communities like Fair Oaks Ranch and Kendall County developments, has attracted families and professionals seeking Hill Country scenery with suburban convenience. The Kendall County Fairgrounds host regular events, and the Boerne Market Days draw thousands on the second Saturday of each month.

Average one-bedroom rents in Boerne are around $1,400, which is slightly above San Antonio city center pricing but reflects the premium schools and Hill Country setting. Newer communities along IH-10 and near the Herff Road corridor list in the $1,500 to $1,800 range.
