Why Richardson?

Richardson's Telecom Corridor is one of the densest concentrations of technology companies in the country. AT&T, Samsung, Cisco, Texas Instruments, and dozens of other tech firms have campuses here, creating a job market that consistently attracts engineers, developers, and tech professionals from across the country. The University of Texas at Dallas adds a research university campus with over 30,000 students, bringing energy and intellectual diversity to the city.

Richardson's CityLine development has created a genuine urban center within the suburb, with DART Red Line access, walkable restaurants, and modern apartment communities built around a central park. At an average one-bedroom rent of $1,350 per month, Richardson is priced competitively against neighboring Plano while offering direct DART rail service that Plano lacks. This makes Richardson particularly attractive for professionals who want transit access to downtown Dallas without paying downtown prices.

The city's diverse dining scene, particularly along its Asian restaurant corridors, is one of the best in DFW. Richardson ISD schools rank highly, and the community has a mature, established feel compared to the still-under-construction edges of Frisco or McKinney.
