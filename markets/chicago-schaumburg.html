Why Schaumburg?

Schaumburg is the commercial heart of Chicago's northwest suburbs, anchored by Woodfield Mall, one of the largest shopping centers in the United States. The Woodfield area has evolved beyond retail into a major corporate corridor with offices for Zurich Insurance, Motorola Solutions, Paylocity, and dozens of technology and financial services companies. This concentration of employers means many Schaumburg residents can live and work in the same area without commuting into the city, though I-90 provides a direct route to downtown Chicago in about 40 minutes outside rush hour.

Schaumburg Township District 54 serves the community's elementary and middle school students, while Schaumburg High School in District 211 is well-regarded. The village has invested heavily in recreation, including the Schaumburg Boomers minor league baseball stadium, the Prairie Center for the Arts, and an extensive park district with golf courses, indoor pools, and sports complexes. The Metra Milwaukee District West line stops at nearby Roselle and Itasca stations for commuter rail access. Schaumburg also benefits from proximity to O'Hare International Airport, making it popular with frequent business travelers.

Average one-bedroom rents in Schaumburg are around $1,400, with newer communities near the Woodfield corridor listing in the $1,600 to $1,900 range. Older communities along Schaumburg Road and near the Metra stations offer more affordable options.
