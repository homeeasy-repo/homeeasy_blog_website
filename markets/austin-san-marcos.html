Why San Marcos?

San Marcos sits at the midpoint of the I-35 corridor between Austin and San Antonio, making it one of the most strategically located cities in Central Texas. Texas State University, with an enrollment of over 38,000 students, drives the local economy and gives the city a vibrant, youthful energy with restaurants, bars, and live music venues concentrated along the downtown square. The San Marcos River, fed by crystal-clear springs, is one of the most popular tubing and kayaking destinations in the state and runs right through the heart of the city.

The San Marcos Premium Outlets and Tanger Outlets together form one of the largest outlet shopping destinations in the country, drawing visitors from across Texas and creating thousands of retail and hospitality jobs. Amazon has opened a major fulfillment center nearby, and the city's location has attracted logistics and distribution companies looking for access to both Austin and San Antonio markets. San Marcos CISD serves the local community, and the city has invested in new parks, trail systems, and a river walk that connects the university to downtown.

Average one-bedroom rents in San Marcos are around $1,100, making it one of the most affordable options in the Austin-San Antonio corridor. Student-oriented complexes near campus can dip below $900 for shared units, while newer conventional communities along IH-35 list in the $1,200 to $1,500 range.
