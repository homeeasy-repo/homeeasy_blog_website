Why Oak Park?

Oak Park is one of Chicago's most architecturally significant suburbs, home to the world's largest collection of Frank Lloyd Wright-designed buildings and the birthplace of Ernest Hemingway. The village's tree-lined streets showcase Prairie-style homes, Victorian houses, and Art Deco apartments that give every block a sense of history and character. But Oak Park is not a museum -- it is a vibrant, progressive community with an active arts scene, diverse population, and a village center full of independent restaurants and shops.

Transit access is a major advantage. The CTA Green Line and Blue Line both serve Oak Park, putting the Loop within a 20-minute train ride. Metra's Union Pacific West line adds another commuter option. This dual CTA access makes Oak Park one of the best-connected suburbs in the entire Chicago metro. At an average one-bedroom rent of $1,200 per month, Oak Park is significantly more affordable than similar transit-rich neighborhoods in Chicago like Lincoln Park or Wicker Park, while offering comparable walkability and a stronger sense of community.

Oak Park's rental market includes charming vintage walk-ups with hardwood floors and crown molding alongside modern apartment buildings near the Austin Boulevard corridor.
