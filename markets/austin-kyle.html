Why Kyle?

Kyle has been one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States, with its population more than tripling in the past decade. The reason is straightforward: Kyle offers some of the most affordable rents in the Austin metro with direct IH-35 access to south Austin, downtown, and the tech corridors. The city sits about 20 miles south of downtown Austin, and the expanding SH-45 and SH-130 connections have shortened commute times to the southeast Austin industrial and tech zone where Tesla's Gigafactory and Samsung's new fab are creating thousands of jobs.

Hays CISD serves Kyle and has invested heavily in new schools to keep pace with growth, including multiple new campuses in the past few years. The Kyle Town Center development has brought H-E-B Plus, Target, and a growing roster of restaurants to the community, reducing the need to drive to Austin for everyday shopping. Plum Creek, one of Kyle's largest master-planned communities, offers a golf course, trails, and community pools. The city's annual Pie in the Sky Hot Air Balloon Festival has become a signature local event, and the revitalizing downtown along Center Street adds small-town charm.

Average one-bedroom rents in Kyle run about $1,250, which is significantly lower than apartments inside Austin city limits. New construction along the IH-35 corridor and near Kyle Crossing lists in the $1,350 to $1,600 range with modern amenities and generous square footage.
