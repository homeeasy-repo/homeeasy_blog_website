Why Cedar Park?

Cedar Park sits northwest of Austin along the US-183 corridor and has grown from a quiet bedroom community into a vibrant suburb with its own identity. The H-E-B Center at Cedar Park hosts concerts, the Texas Stars (AHL hockey), and the Austin Spurs (NBA G League), giving residents entertainment options without driving into downtown Austin. The 1890 Ranch shopping center offers big-box retail and dining, and the Brushy Creek trail system connects neighborhoods with miles of hike and bike paths through greenbelt corridors.

Leander ISD serves most of Cedar Park and is one of the fastest-growing school districts in Texas, consistently earning high marks for academic performance. The tech corridor along US-183 and the 183A toll road has attracted employers like Vista Equity Partners, National Instruments, and a number of smaller tech companies, meaning many Cedar Park residents can work locally. The commute to downtown Austin runs about 25 to 30 minutes on 183, or faster via the MoPac toll lane system. Cedar Park also borders Lake Travis to the west, giving residents easy access to lake recreation on weekends.

Average one-bedroom rents in Cedar Park are around $1,450, with newer construction along the 183A toll road listing in the $1,600 to $1,900 range.
