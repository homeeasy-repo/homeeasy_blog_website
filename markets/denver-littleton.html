Why Littleton?

Littleton's historic Main Street is one of the best-preserved and most vibrant downtown districts in the Denver metro. Independent bookshops, farm-to-table restaurants, art galleries, and craft breweries line the street, creating a walkable village feel that draws visitors from across the south metro. The RTD C and D light rail lines serve Littleton, providing direct access to downtown Denver, the Tech Center, and Lone Tree without a car. The Mineral light rail station area has attracted transit-oriented apartment development that lets you commute by train while living in a community with genuine small-town character.

Littleton's outdoor recreation access is exceptional even by Colorado standards. The South Platte River Trail runs through the city, connecting to Chatfield State Park's reservoir and 26 miles of trails. The Highline Canal Trail provides another multi-use corridor for biking and walking. Roxborough State Park, with its dramatic red rock formations, is a short drive south. Littleton Public Schools are well-regarded, and the community has a mature, established feel. At an average one-bedroom rent of $1,350 per month, Littleton offers remarkable quality of life relative to its price point.

Littleton's compact size means rental inventory is more limited than larger suburbs like Aurora or Lakewood. Our AI monitors availability and pricing changes to help you act quickly when a well-priced apartment opens up in this desirable community.
