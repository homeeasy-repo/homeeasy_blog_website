The Market

River North is Chicago's nightlife and entertainment district. Galleries, rooftop bars, Michigan Avenue shopping. It's the neighborhood everyone pictures when they think "downtown Chicago living." We track 104 buildings here — 81 of which are in our portfolio directly.

With 81 buildings, concession activity is widespread. The most "popular" neighborhood in Chicago and buildings are still competing for tenants. Read into that — it means vacancy pressure is real, even here. Buildings don't offer months free when they're full.

The price range is unusually wide for a neighborhood with this reputation. Rent starts at $1,324 and runs up to $4,280, with some top-floor units pushing past $9,400. That low end surprises people. River North is not exclusively a $3,000+ neighborhood — not if you know where to look.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

81 buildings — deep selection across every price point from $1,324 to $9,400+. Walkable to Michigan Avenue, the gallery district, and hundreds of restaurants and bars. The building stock spans 1901 to 2024 — vintage conversions, 1980s high-rises, and brand new towers.

What You Give Up

Quiet. The entertainment is the selling point and the problem. Weekend nights are loud — bar crowds on the sidewalks, rideshares double-parked, noise until 3am. The tourist density is higher than any other residential neighborhood. If you're a light sleeper or you work early Saturday mornings, the bar crowd is not your neighbor of choice.

What's Happening in the Market

River North has deep concession activity across our 81 buildings — months free, waived admin fees, look-and-lease specials. That's unusual for a neighborhood this well-known. It tells you that supply has outpaced demand, even in the most popular zip code.

The building stock here spans over a century. Early 1900s warehouse conversions sit next to 1980s high-rises and 2020s glass towers. That variety is part of what creates the wide price range. A converted loft from 1981 prices differently than a brand new tower from 2024, and both exist on the same block.

Credit requirements vary widely with that building age. Newer luxury towers tend to want 680+. Older buildings are more flexible. If your credit is below 650, there are still buildings here that will work — you just need to know which ones.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,500/mo: Yes, River North. Starting at $1,324. These are older buildings and smaller units, but the zip code is the same. Not every River North apartment costs $3,000.

$1,500–$2,000/mo: Asbury Plaza is the standout — $1,993 start, $2.95/sqft, built 1981. That's a steal for River North. At this price point you're looking at 1980s high-rises with solid bones and working amenities. Not new finishes, but real value.

$2,000–$2,500/mo: The options open up. 100 W Chestnut ($2,340), Grand Plaza ($2,309), Flair Tower ($2,495). Mix of eras — 1983, 2003, 2018. Concessions at this level can drop effective rent by $150–$250/mo.

$2,500–$4,000/mo: The bulk of River North inventory. Newer towers, updated finishes, rooftop pools, in-unit laundry. With 81 buildings in our portfolio, this is where you have the most negotiating room.

$4,000+: Top-floor units, 2- and 3-bedrooms, the newest construction. Ceiling goes to $9,400+. At this level the concessions save real money — 1 month free on a $4,200 unit is $350/mo off your effective rent.

Getting Around

Brown and Purple lines at Merchandise Mart. Red Line at Grand and Chicago stations. The Loop is a 10-minute walk south. Michigan Avenue is right there. The lakefront trail is a short walk east — runners and cyclists don't have to work for it.

Groceries: Whole Foods on Huron, Trader Joe's on Ontario. Both walkable. Dining is not a problem — River North has more restaurants per block than anywhere in Chicago.

Who Does Well Here

Nightlife people. Young professionals who want to be in the center of everything. People who eat out five nights a week and don't mind paying for the location. People who want the most options — 81 buildings means we have something at nearly every price point.

If you want quiet evenings and early bedtimes, Gold Coast is a block north and a different world. If you want the same energy at a lower price, check South Loop ($3.90/sqft, 102 buildings). But if River North is where you want to be — we have 81 buildings to work with.
