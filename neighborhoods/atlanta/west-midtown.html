The Market

West Midtown is Atlanta's warehouse-to-loft conversion district. 46 total buildings mapped, 35 in our portfolio. The neighborhood sits west of the Downtown Connector (I-75/I-85), roughly bounded by Howell Mill Road and Marietta Street. A property manager talks about their one building. We talk about 35 of them.

The data tells us West Midtown has been the fastest-developing submarket in Atlanta over the past five years. The Works -- a 75-acre mixed-use development -- is the anchor project, joining Star Metals, Westside Provisions District, and the Interlock. The area was industrial and empty a decade ago. Now it is restaurants, breweries, coworking spaces, and new apartment construction. The Westside BeltLine connector is under development, which will link this neighborhood to the broader BeltLine system. That infrastructure play means pricing here is still shifting as new projects deliver.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Converted warehouse aesthetic alongside new construction. The Works, Star Metals, Westside Provisions District, the Interlock. Atlanta's best restaurant concentration outside Buckhead -- Bacchanalia, Cooks & Soldiers, JCT Kitchen. Brewery scene along Howell Mill. Industrial-creative neighborhood character. Relatively competitive pricing for ITP (inside the perimeter) Atlanta. 35 buildings with a mix of loft conversions and new mid-rises.

What You Give Up

No MARTA rail access -- this is a car-dependent neighborhood. The Westside BeltLine connector is under construction but not yet complete. Active development means construction noise and dust. Howell Mill Road traffic is heavy, especially around restaurant clusters on weekends. The neighborhood character is still forming -- some blocks are polished mixed-use, others are still empty lots or active construction sites. Walkability is improving but not yet on par with Midtown or OFW.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,600/mo: Older warehouse conversions or buildings on the periphery of the development corridor. Smaller loft-style units with character. Limited options as more new construction fills in.

$1,600-$2,200/mo: The core West Midtown inventory. New mid-rise construction with amenity packages. Star Metals-adjacent, The Works-adjacent, Westside Provisions walkability. Most of the 35 buildings fall in this range.

$2,200+/mo: Top-tier new construction with premium finishes. Penthouse-level loft conversions. Direct walkability to the restaurant corridor. The question at this price point is whether you want the extra space of a loft conversion or the polish of new construction.

Getting Around

West Midtown has no MARTA rail stations. Bus routes along Marietta Street and Northside Drive provide some transit, but this is a car-first neighborhood. Howell Mill Road is the main commercial artery -- restaurants, retail, and The Works cluster along it. The Westside BeltLine connector will eventually link West Midtown to the broader trail network, but that is still under development. Bike lanes exist along some streets. Within the core restaurant and retail cluster, walking works. For commuting to Midtown or Downtown, you need a car, Uber, or the bus -- a 10-15 minute drive depending on traffic.
