The Market

Buckhead has the highest building count in Atlanta. 96 total buildings mapped, 76 in our portfolio. That is 8.5% of the entire Atlanta metro inventory in one submarket. Buckhead runs from Peachtree Road north through Lenox Road to the Buckhead Village district. A property manager talks about their one building. We talk about 76 of them.

The data tells us Buckhead is Atlanta's premium tier. Peachtree Road is lined with high-rises -- this is where the financial district bleeds into upscale residential. Lenox Square and Phipps Plaza anchor the retail core. The Buckhead Village district has been redeveloped into a walkable mixed-use center. Governor's Mansion sits here. The Atlanta Financial Center and several major law and consulting firms drive weekday demand. MARTA's Buckhead and Lenox stations provide rail access, though Buckhead is more car-dependent than Midtown.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Deepest inventory in Atlanta -- 76 buildings. Upscale retail at Lenox Square and Phipps Plaza. Financial district proximity. High-rise options with views. Buckhead Village walkable district. Two MARTA stations. Strong restaurant and nightlife scene along Peachtree. Governor's Mansion neighborhood for a premium residential feel. Well-maintained streetscapes.

What You Give Up

Premium pricing -- Buckhead is generally the most expensive submarket in Atlanta. Car-dependent despite MARTA stations -- daily errands still require driving for most residents. Peachtree Road traffic is slow during rush hour. Less neighborhood character than BeltLine-adjacent areas. If you want gritty walkability or converted warehouse vibes, this is not the place.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,800/mo: Older garden-style or mid-rise buildings on side streets off Peachtree. These exist but inventory is limited at this price point. Typically smaller units without the amenity packages of newer construction.

$1,800-$2,500/mo: The core of Buckhead's rental market. Newer mid-rise and high-rise buildings with pools, fitness centers, coworking. Lenox Road and Peachtree Road frontage. This range covers the majority of the 76 buildings.

$2,500+/mo: Top-floor high-rise units with skyline views. Buckhead Village proximity. Concierge buildings with premium amenity packages. The question is whether view or square footage matters more -- both rarely come together under $3,000.

Getting Around

MARTA serves Buckhead at two stations: Buckhead and Lenox. The Red line runs north-south. Bus routes along Peachtree and Lenox supplement rail. Despite transit access, Buckhead is more car-dependent than Midtown -- the neighborhood is spread out and daily errands (groceries, dry cleaning) often require a car. Peachtree Road has sidewalks but is not a pedestrian-first street. Uber and Lyft are heavily used for nightlife in the Buckhead Village area. Parking is more available and less expensive than Midtown, with most buildings including one space.
