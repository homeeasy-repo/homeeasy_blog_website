The Market

South Congress is Austin's most iconic street. The vintage shops, the food trucks, the "I love you so much" mural, the Congress Avenue Bridge bats at sunset. It's the postcard version of Austin, and for good reason — it genuinely has character that most of this city's newer developments can't replicate.

We track 22 buildings in the South Congress area, 17 in our portfolio. That is a solid network for a neighborhood that was historically dominated by vintage bungalows, duplexes, and small rental properties. The inventory has expanded significantly as new construction has come online along the corridor.

The buildings span a wide range: The Brook Apartments starts at $850 ($1.55/sqft) and The Lola starts at $1,903 ($2.87/sqft). That gap reflects the mix of old Austin and new Austin that coexists along this corridor.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Austin's most iconic street. Walkable, vibrant, full of character. Vintage shops, food trucks, live music, the Congress Avenue Bridge bats. Lady Bird Lake is right there. The closest thing to a "Main Street" Austin has. You can walk to downtown across the bridge.

What You Give Up

Tourist foot traffic is heavy on weekends. Parking is a headache. The inventory spans a wide range from very affordable (The Brook at $850) to newer/premium (The Lola at $1,903).

Getting Around

South Congress itself is highly walkable — shops, restaurants, and bars line the street, and you can walk across the Congress Avenue Bridge to downtown in 15 minutes. Lady Bird Lake's hike-and-bike trail is immediately accessible. CapMetro bus routes run along Congress Avenue.

Beyond the immediate corridor, a car helps. But if your daily life revolves around SoCo, downtown, and the Lady Bird Lake trail, you can make it work without one.
