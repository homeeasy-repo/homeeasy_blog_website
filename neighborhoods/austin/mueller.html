The Market

Mueller is built on the site of Austin's old Robert Mueller Municipal Airport. The city tore down the runways and master-planned an entire neighborhood from scratch — parks, retail, walking paths, a farmers market, the Thinkery children's museum. The result is something Austin almost never has: a walkable, mixed-use neighborhood that wasn't designed for cars.

At $2.75/sqft, Mueller sits in the middle of the Austin price spectrum — 24% cheaper than downtown ($3.61) but 33% above The Domain ($2.07). Rent runs from $1,506 to $2,055, with a median of $1,786. That's a tight range, which makes sense for a master-planned development where everything was built around the same time.

3 of 12 buildings are running concessions. With 13 buildings tracked in the area, your options are well-defined and you know exactly what you're choosing between.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

A walkable master-planned community in a city that has almost none. Parks, a farmers market, the Thinkery museum, retail and restaurants — all designed to be accessed on foot. Central location near UT campus and downtown. Well-maintained, cohesive neighborhood feel.

What You Give Up

The neighborhood feels designed, not organic. Compared to East Austin's gritty character or downtown's urban energy, Mueller can feel like a nice suburban development that happens to be centrally located. Not cheap at $2.75/sqft for what is essentially a planned community.

What's Happening in the Market

Mueller is essentially built out. The development plan is mature, which means what you see is what you get — no new towers coming to dramatically change the market or add inventory. That's both good and bad. Good: the neighborhood is stable and predictable. Bad: limited supply means less competition among buildings and fewer concession opportunities.

The 3 active concessions suggest some softness. If you like what Mueller offers, move while concessions are available.

Mueller's appeal is specific: families, people who want to walk to the park and the farmers market, people who like that everything is clean and well-organized. If that's you, this is one of the few places in Austin that delivers it without a car.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,600/mo: Overture Mueller at $2.45/sqft from $1,506 — the best value in Mueller. Active concession. Mosaic at Mueller also starts at $1,565. Both are your entry points into this neighborhood.

$1,600–$2,000/mo: The core of Mueller's market. Most of the 12 buildings price in this range. You're looking at modern construction with the standard amenity package — pools, fitness centers, walkable access to Mueller's parks and retail.

$2,000+: Larger units and premium floorplans. At this budget, also seriously compare The Domain ($2.07/sqft) and South Lamar ($2.42/sqft) — you'll get more space for similar or less money, with different but comparable neighborhood amenities.

Getting Around

Mueller was designed for walkability, and it shows. The internal layout puts retail, restaurants, parks, and the farmers market within walking distance of the residential areas. Bike paths connect to the broader Austin trail network. CapMetro bus routes serve the area.

For commuting beyond Mueller, you'll likely need a car. It's centrally located — about 10 minutes to downtown, 15 to The Domain — but Austin's public transit doesn't make cross-city trips practical. The walkability is real within Mueller; outside it, you're back in car-dependent Austin.

Who Does Well Here

Families. People who want a farmers market and a park more than a bar scene. People relocating from walkable cities who need that lifestyle but with more space and lower prices than downtown. Anyone who values planning, maintenance, and predictability.

If you want nightlife and creative energy, East Austin or downtown is a better fit. If you want the suburban feel of Cedar Park but with a central location and walkability, Mueller splits the difference.
