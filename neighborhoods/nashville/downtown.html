The Market

Downtown Nashville and SoBro (South of Broadway) is the core. 21 buildings mapped, 17 in our portfolio -- an 81% portfolio coverage. These are predominantly high-rise and mid-rise towers built in the last decade, clustered around Broadway, the Music City Center convention complex, and the Cumberland River waterfront.

The data tells us Downtown/SoBro is Nashville's premium tier. You are paying for proximity to everything that makes Nashville Nashville -- Lower Broadway honky-tonks, Bridgestone Arena, Nissan Stadium, the entertainment district. The trade-off is noise, tourist density, and pricing that reflects the address. New supply keeps delivering here, which means concession cycles are real. The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Walking distance to everything. Broadway entertainment. Bridgestone Arena. Nissan Stadium across the river. Convention center access. Newest construction in Nashville. High-rise views of the skyline and Cumberland River. Restaurants, bars, and nightlife at your doorstep. No car needed for daily life.

What You Give Up

Tourist noise -- Broadway is loud, especially weekends. Premium pricing for the address. Parking costs are high if you keep a car. Limited grocery options within walking distance. The neighborhood empties out of locals on weekend nights when tourists take over. Less neighborhood character than Germantown or East Nashville.

Getting Around

Downtown Nashville is walkable within its core. The WeGo public transit hub connects bus routes across the metro. The Music City Star commuter rail runs from Riverfront Station to the eastern suburbs. Scooters and bikes are everywhere. I-40 and I-65 converge here, so driving out of downtown is straightforward -- getting into downtown during events is the harder part. A car is useful for reaching neighborhoods outside the core, but not necessary for daily life downtown.
