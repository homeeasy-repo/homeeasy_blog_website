The Market

Hillsboro Village is a compact, walkable neighborhood wedged between Vanderbilt University and Belmont University. 11 buildings mapped, 8 in our portfolio. The inventory here is tight by design -- the neighborhood's scale is small, the zoning is constrained, and the demand from two major universities plus Vanderbilt University Medical Center keeps occupancy high.

The data tells us Hillsboro Village is a demand-constrained market. Unlike The Gulch or Germantown, where new supply is constantly delivering and creating concession cycles, Hillsboro Village does not have room for large new developments. What that means for renters: less concession activity, but also a stable, walkable neighborhood with a clear identity. The 21st Avenue South commercial strip -- Pancake Pantry, Fido, Blvd Nashville, Belcourt Theatre -- is one of Nashville's best pedestrian corridors.

Getting Around

Hillsboro Village is walkable within its core. 21st Avenue South is the commercial spine running from Vanderbilt south toward Belmont. Blakemore Avenue connects west to Centennial Park. WeGo bus routes run along 21st and Hillsboro Pike. Vanderbilt's campus is a 5-minute walk. Downtown is about 20 minutes by car or bus, further than The Gulch or Germantown. A car is useful for reaching the broader metro, but daily life in Hillsboro Village can work on foot.
