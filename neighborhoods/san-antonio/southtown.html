The Market

Southtown is San Antonio's arts district. First Friday art walks draw crowds from across the city. South Alamo Street has restaurants, craft cocktail bars, and taquerias. The Blue Star Arts Complex anchors the gallery scene. It is walkable to downtown, adjacent to historic King William, and has the kind of neighborhood energy that money cannot manufacture.

Southtown has 4 buildings tracked in our system. Similar to King William next door, the housing stock is a mix of renovated historic homes, converted warehouse lofts, and small-scale developments. Newer mid-rise construction has arrived as the neighborhood's popularity has grown, and our inventory now covers the main apartment options in the area. For deeper selection, the downtown core just north has 30 buildings.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

First Friday art walks. South Alamo Street dining -- farm-to-table, craft cocktails, neighborhood taquerias. Blue Star Arts Complex. Walkable to downtown. The River Walk's Mission Reach runs through for cycling and walking. Real neighborhood character with vintage and contemporary sitting side by side. The St. Mary's Strip for live music.

What You Give Up

Small neighborhood with 4 buildings -- good coverage for the area but limited total selection. Units move fast in this popular arts district. For deeper selection, look at downtown (30 buildings, 10-minute walk) or Stone Oak (20 buildings).

Getting Around

Southtown has a Walk Score around 75. South Alamo and South St. Mary's Streets provide walkable restaurant and shop access. The Mission Reach section of the River Walk connects Southtown to downtown and the historic missions. VIA bus routes serve the main streets. Downtown is a short walk or bike ride north across the river. A car is useful but not essential if you live along the main corridors.

Who Does Well Here

Artists, young professionals, and anyone who prioritizes neighborhood character and walkable dining. With 4 buildings, Southtown now has usable locator inventory for the arts district.
