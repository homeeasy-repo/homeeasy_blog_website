The Market

Alamo Heights has 3 buildings in our portfolio. Sunset Ridge, at $1.56/sqft starting from $1,200, is one of them with an active concession.

Alamo Heights is an affluent enclave -- technically its own independent city within San Antonio, known locally as "09" for its 78209 zip code. The housing stock is overwhelmingly single-family homes. Large-scale apartment complexes are rare here because the zoning and community character limit them. Most renters in Alamo Heights are in garage apartments, duplexes, or small-scale conversions rented directly by homeowners.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Affluent, tree-lined streets with genuine neighborhood character. Top-rated Alamo Heights ISD -- among the best public schools in Texas. The Broadway corridor has walkable restaurants, coffee shops, and boutiques. McNay Art Museum and Brackenridge Park are within the neighborhood. Quiet residential streets that feel like a small town inside a big city.

What You Give Up

3 buildings in a homeowner area. Selection is limited compared to other SA neighborhoods. For more options, look at the Pearl District (20 buildings, 10 minutes south) or Stone Oak (20 buildings, 15 minutes north).

Getting Around

The Broadway corridor provides walkable access to restaurants and shops. Brackenridge Park and the San Antonio Zoo are within walking distance. VIA bus routes serve the main roads. A car is useful but not mandatory if you live near Broadway. Alamo Heights connects to downtown SA in about 10 minutes by car.

Who Does Well Here

Families who prioritize the Alamo Heights school district above all else. Professionals who want established neighborhood character over new construction amenities. With 3 buildings, we have options here, plus direct-to-landlord garage apartments and small conversions. If you need more selection and concessions, look at the Pearl or Stone Oak -- but Alamo Heights is one of the best places to live in San Antonio.
