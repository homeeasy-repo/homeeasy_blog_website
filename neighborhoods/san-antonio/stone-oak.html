The Market

Stone Oak is where the math works best in San Antonio. 20 buildings in our portfolio. New construction. $1.64/sqft median. Entry at $886. Nearly half the buildings running concessions. If your priority is getting the newest apartment with the most space for the least money, this is the neighborhood.

The trade-off is location. Stone Oak sits in far north San Antonio along US-281 and Loop 1604 -- about 25 minutes from downtown without traffic, longer during rush hour. This is master-planned suburban living. You need a car. There is no walkability to speak of. But the apartment stock is almost entirely post-2010 construction, which means modern floor plans, in-unit laundry, granite counters, and full amenity packages as standard. 9 of 20 buildings with active concessions tells you these buildings are competing hard for residents.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

New construction at low prices. 20 buildings means real selection -- you can comparison shop. 9 with concessions. Modern amenities as standard. Family-oriented with good schools in the NEISD system. Hill Country scenery. Eisenhower Park nearby for hiking.

What You Give Up

Suburban. Car required for everything. 25+ minutes to downtown. No walkability -- shopping and dining are clustered at major intersections, not along walkable streets. If you work downtown, the commute will define your daily life.

What's Happening in the Market

Stone Oak has been in a building cycle for over a decade. New complexes keep coming online, which drives competition between buildings. When 9 of 20 buildings are running concessions, that is market dynamics working in your favor -- buildings that need to fill units give away months free, waive admin fees, or offer look-and-lease specials.

Montecristo at $1.22/sqft from $886 is the floor. The Ravinia at $1.28/sqft from $988 is the next step up. Both are well below the neighborhood median, meaning even within Stone Oak there is a wide value range. The best deals go to renters who are flexible on exact location within the Stone Oak area and willing to sign longer leases.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,000/mo: Montecristo from $886, The Ravinia from $988. New construction, full amenities. This is under $1,000 for a modern apartment in a major American city. Concessions can push effective rent even lower.

$1,000-$1,300/mo: The median band. Multiple buildings competing at this price point. Expect in-unit laundry, pools, fitness centers, updated finishes. This is where most Stone Oak renters land.

$1,300-$1,792/mo: Top of range. Larger 2-bedroom units, premium finishes, top-floor locations. At $1,792 in Stone Oak you are getting what $2,500+ buys in most Texas downtowns.

Getting Around

Car required. Walk Score is around 30. US-281 and Loop 1604 are your main arteries. Shopping, dining, and services cluster at major intersections -- Stone Oak Parkway, The Rim, La Cantera. VIA bus service exists but is limited. If you work in the Stone Oak medical corridor or at nearby military installations, the commute is short. If you work downtown, budget 25-40 minutes each way.

Who Does Well Here

Families who want new construction and good schools at a price that leaves room in the budget. Military personnel at nearby bases. Healthcare workers at the Stone Oak medical corridor. Anyone who prioritizes apartment quality and price over walkability and nightlife. If you need to be in the action, look at the Pearl District or Downtown. If you want the best apartment for the money, Stone Oak is it.
