The Market

52 buildings tracked in Victory Park, 32 in our portfolio. That's real inventory in a pocket most people associate only with the arena.

Trinity Loft starts at $1,495 ($1.80/sqft). Trend Design District starts at $1,093 ($1.89/sqft). At $1.80–$1.89/sqft on the value end, Victory Park is one of the cheaper pockets in central Dallas — well below Uptown ($2.80) and Downtown ($2.51). With 32 buildings, there's genuine selection here.

The neighborhood is the American Airlines Center and the development surrounding it. If Mavs games, Stars games, and concerts are part of your weekly routine, being able to walk home from the arena has real value. That's not a lifestyle pitch — it's a transportation savings calculation.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Walking distance to the American Airlines Center. 32 buildings at fair prices. More square footage per dollar than Uptown or Downtown. Adjacent to Uptown (115 buildings) and Downtown (68 buildings) for everything else you need.

What You Give Up

The neighborhood itself is event-driven — it gets lively on game nights and quiet the rest of the time. No grocery stores within walking distance. This is an arena district, not a full neighborhood.

What's Happening in the Market

With 32 buildings in the Victory Park area, there's enough competition to find concessions and room to negotiate.

Victory Park is sandwiched between Uptown and Downtown Dallas. If nothing here works, you're not stuck. Uptown has 115 buildings in our portfolio. Downtown has 68. Both are a short drive or DART ride away. But with 32 buildings, Victory Park is no longer just a niche bet — it's a real option with AAC proximity at a good price.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,500/mo: Trend Design District starts at $1,093. That's the entry point — you're getting into the Victory Park area at a price point that undercuts most of central Dallas.

$1,500–$2,000/mo: Trinity Loft opens up at $1,495. Both buildings are in play. At this range you're likely looking at a solid 1BR with concessions bringing effective rent even lower.

$2,000+: At this budget, Victory Park is well within reach. You can also consider Uptown (115 buildings) or Downtown (68 buildings) for even more variety.

Getting Around

Victory Park has its own DART station, which connects you to the rest of Dallas. The Katy Trail is accessible nearby for running and cycling. The real draw is walkability to the arena — no $40 Ubers home after a late game. For everything else — groceries, errands, dining variety — you're driving or taking the train to Uptown or Downtown.

Who Does Well Here

Sports fans. People who go to Mavs and Stars games regularly and want to walk home after. People who value price per square foot over neighborhood variety. If you work downtown or in Uptown, the commute is short. If you need a full-service neighborhood with grocery stores, coffee shops, and restaurants on every block — Uptown is right next door and has 115 buildings in our portfolio.

The honest recommendation: look at Victory Park first. If neither building works, expand to Uptown or Downtown. You lose nothing by checking here first — and you might find a better price than anywhere else in central Dallas.
