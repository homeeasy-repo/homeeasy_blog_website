The Market

Uptown is the largest inventory pool in DFW. 206 buildings in our database, 115 in our portfolio — more than any other neighborhood in the metro. That matters because selection means leverage. More buildings competing for tenants means better pricing, more concessions, and more room to negotiate.

At $2.80/sqft median, Uptown is the second most expensive neighborhood in DFW after Knox-Henderson ($3.06). That's the premium for walkability. Dallas is a car city. Uptown is the exception — McKinney Avenue trolley, Katy Trail, restaurants and bars within walking distance. You're paying for something that barely exists anywhere else in the metro.

Rent starts at $854 on the low end and runs to $2,995. The sweet spot for most renters is $1,400-$1,800. With 115 buildings, there are concessions and opportunities across the market if you know where to look.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

The most walkable neighborhood in DFW. McKinney Avenue trolley (free), Katy Trail for running and biking, restaurants and bars you can actually walk to. 115 buildings means the widest selection in the metro — more floor plans, more price points, more leverage.

What You Give Up

Most expensive DFW market after Knox-Henderson. Parking is a pain — street parking is scarce and most buildings charge extra. If cheap rent is the priority, Arlington and Plano will stretch your dollar further. You're paying the walkability tax here.

What's Happening in the Market

Uptown has been building nonstop. That's why you have 206 buildings tracked in one neighborhood — the density is real. New construction keeps coming online, and when supply grows faster than demand, concessions appear.

With 115 buildings, enough are competing that you can shop around. Concessions in Uptown typically look like waived admin fees, a month free on 13-month leases, or look-and-lease specials. They don't last forever — when units fill, the specials go away.

The value play in Uptown is the buildings that don't get the Instagram attention. Everyone knows the new towers on McKinney. Fewer people know about Hall Street Flats at $2.04/sqft or Gateway East at $2.14/sqft. Those pricing gaps exist because the market is big enough to have them.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,200/mo: It exists. Gateway East at Arts District starts at $1,211. That's the entry point to Uptown — not the shiny part of McKinney Avenue, but you're in the neighborhood. At $2.14/sqft, the math actually works.

$1,200–$1,500/mo: Hall Street Flats at $1,485 gives you $2.04/sqft — the best value per square foot in Uptown. You're getting more space for less money than most of the neighborhood. Selection starts to open up in this range.

$1,500–$1,800/mo: The sweet spot. This is where the bulk of Uptown inventory lives. Newer construction, in-unit laundry in most, pools, fitness centers. Mondrian West Village sits here at $1,994 start with $2.16/sqft. Concession-eligible units make this range even more accessible.

$1,800–$2,995: Top-tier Uptown. Newer towers, premium finishes, rooftop views of downtown Dallas. This is where concessions make the biggest dollar impact — a month free on a $2,500 unit drops your effective rent by $200/mo. The high end of the market, but you're getting the full Uptown experience.

Getting Around

The McKinney Avenue trolley runs free through the heart of Uptown and connects to the DART rail system at Cityplace/Uptown station. That gets you downtown in minutes. The Katy Trail — 3.5 miles of converted rail line — runs through the neighborhood and doubles as both commuter path and social scene. Runners, cyclists, dogs, post-work drinks at trailside bars.

Walk Score hovers around 80, which is exceptional for Dallas. You can walk to groceries, restaurants, bars. That said, this is still Texas — a car helps for anything outside the neighborhood. But for day-to-day living, Uptown is as close to car-optional as DFW gets.

Who Does Well Here

People who want to walk places in a city built for cars. Young professionals who want nightlife on their doorstep. People relocating to DFW who don't know the market yet — Uptown is the safe starting point, the neighborhood where you figure out Dallas before committing to a suburb.

If your priority is cheap rent, look at Arlington or Plano. If your priority is space, the suburbs will give you more square footage per dollar. But if you want the one neighborhood in DFW where you can leave your car parked and still have a life, this is it.
