The Market

Arlington is the value play in DFW. That's not a euphemism — it's the data. 65 buildings, a $685 entry point, and a median rent of $1,075/mo. At $1.72/sqft, Arlington is 39% cheaper than Uptown ($2.80) and the cheapest major submarket in the metro.

32 of 65 buildings are running concessions right now. Half the market is offering something — months free, waived fees, reduced deposits. That's vacancy pressure working in your favor. Buildings don't discount when they're full.

Arlington sits between Dallas and Fort Worth, home to AT&T Stadium and Globe Life Field. It's not walkable. You need a car for everything. But if your budget matters more than your commute, this is where your dollar stretches furthest in DFW.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Cheapest major submarket in DFW. 65 buildings to choose from — 2nd largest inventory in the metro. Half running concessions. $685 entry point. More square footage per dollar than anywhere else. AT&T Stadium and Globe Life Field are in your backyard.

What You Give Up

Car-dependent. Zero walkability. No rail transit — Arlington opted out of DART. 20-30 minute drive to Dallas or Fort Worth. No nightlife scene to speak of. Dining is suburban chains and strip-mall restaurants. If you want urban energy, this is not it.

What's Happening in the Market

Arlington's rental market is soft right now. When half your buildings are running concessions, that tells you supply is outpacing demand. For renters, that's a good thing — it means leverage.

The entertainment district around the stadiums keeps drawing development, but the residential market hasn't caught up in demand. Result: newer buildings offering aggressive move-in specials to fill units. Months free, waived admin fees, reduced deposits.

Credit requirements tend to be more flexible here than in Dallas's urban core. Budget renters, Section 8 voucher holders, and anyone with imperfect credit will find more doors open in Arlington than in Uptown or Knox-Henderson.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $800/mo: Rise North Arlington at $685 — $1.03/sqft, the cheapest entry point in all of DFW. Not new, not flashy. But the math works. At this price, you're paying less than some people's car payments.

$800–$1,100/mo: Your options open up significantly. Maxwell ($835 start), Elliot Windsprint ($932 start), and a dozen more buildings in this range. This is the median price band — the heart of Arlington's market.

$1,100–$1,500/mo: The sweet spot for space. Two-bedrooms become available. Newer construction with in-unit washer/dryers, pools, fitness centers. Concessions at this tier can knock $100-200/mo off effective rent.

$1,500+: Top-tier Arlington. Larger two-bedrooms and three-bedrooms in newer communities. At this budget in Arlington, you're getting what $2,500+ buys in Uptown — just without the walkability.

Getting Around

You need a car. That's not a suggestion — it's a requirement. Arlington is the largest city in the US without a mass transit rail system. They voted against joining DART, and it shows. I-30 and I-20 are your lifelines to Dallas and Fort Worth, with 20-40 minute commutes depending on traffic and direction.

The upside of car-dependent: free parking everywhere. Wide streets. No circling the block for 20 minutes. Your apartment complex will have a spot for you, usually covered, usually included.

Who Does Well Here

People who prioritize budget over lifestyle. People who work in Arlington, or midway between Dallas and Fort Worth. Families who need space — a 2BR here costs what a studio costs in Knox-Henderson. Sports fans who want to walk to Cowboys and Rangers games instead of paying $40 to park.

If you need nightlife, walkability, or urban energy — look at Uptown, Deep Ellum, or Knox-Henderson. If you need the most apartment for the least money in DFW, this is it.
