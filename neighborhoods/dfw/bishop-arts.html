The Neighborhood

Bishop Arts is south of downtown Dallas, centered around Bishop Avenue and Davis Street. It's the kind of neighborhood that gets called "quirky" and "artsy" — and for once, those labels are accurate. Independent boutiques, art galleries, locally-owned restaurants, coffee shops that aren't chains. The food scene is diverse: Mexican, Italian, barbecue, vegan, all within a few walkable blocks.

This is not a high-rise neighborhood. Most of the housing stock is small landlord walk-ups, renovated duplexes, and older apartment buildings. The kind of places that fill units by word of mouth and Craigslist. That's why our portfolio has zero buildings here.

If you want to live in Bishop Arts specifically, you're going direct to small landlords. That means no concessions we can negotiate, no pricing data we can benchmark, and no locator service. It's a different kind of apartment search — more legwork, less data.

Getting Around

Bishop Arts is walkable within the neighborhood itself — the commercial blocks along Bishop Avenue and Davis Street are compact and pedestrian-friendly. Beyond the neighborhood, you're driving. DART has a stop at Bishop Arts, but service frequency is limited. Downtown Dallas is a 10-minute drive north. The highways (I-35E, I-30) are close for longer commutes.

Groceries: El Rio Grande Latin Market is nearby. For a full grocery run, you're driving to a Kroger or Walmart in Oak Cliff or north toward downtown.

Who Does Well Here

People who prioritize neighborhood character over building amenities. People who'd rather have a local taco spot downstairs than a rooftop pool upstairs.
