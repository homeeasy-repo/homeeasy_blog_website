The Market

Knox-Henderson is the most expensive pocket of the entire DFW metroplex by price per square foot. At $3.06/sqft median, it costs more than Uptown ($2.80), more than Downtown Dallas ($2.51), more than everywhere. That's the data.

Why? Henderson Avenue. It's the best dining and bar strip in Dallas outside of Deep Ellum — and it draws a crowd willing to pay a premium to live within walking distance. 37 buildings tracked, 21 in our portfolio — and this is a neighborhood where demand stays strong.

Rent ranges from $1,620 to $2,217, with a median of $1,800. The $/sqft spread is $2.33 to $3.57. That top end — $3.57 — is a number you'd expect in Chicago, not Dallas. You're paying for location, not square footage.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

The best dining and nightlife strip in Dallas outside Deep Ellum. Henderson Avenue is walkable, well-lit, and packed with restaurants, bars, and patios. Small enough that it feels like a neighborhood, not a district. Close to Highland Park, the Katy Trail, and Uptown.

What You Give Up

The most expensive $/sqft in all of DFW. This is a tight market where buildings don't need to compete on price. If your budget is firm, you get significantly less space here than anywhere else.

What's Happening in the Market

Knox-Henderson buildings don't need to offer deals because the demand is there. Henderson Avenue has hit a tipping point where the restaurant scene is self-sustaining — new spots open, regulars keep coming, the walkability creates its own gravity.

The $/sqft spread from $2.33 to $3.57 tells you there's still a range. Slate at Cole Park and The Henderson sit at the lower end — they're your best value plays in a premium market. The buildings at $3.50+ are charging a pure location premium.

If you want to be in this exact area but the pricing doesn't work, Oak Lawn is the move. It's immediately adjacent, has 60 buildings in our portfolio, and the median $/sqft is $2.05. That's a 33% price drop for a neighborhood that's a 5-minute drive from Henderson Avenue.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,700/mo: Knox-Henderson is out of reach. The lowest starting rent in our portfolio here is $1,620, and that's for the smallest units. At this budget, look at Deep Ellum ($2.33/sqft) or Oak Lawn ($2.05/sqft) for similar vibes at lower prices.

$1,700–$2,000/mo: Your options open — barely. Slate at Cole Park ($1,749) and The Henderson ($1,737) are the value plays. These are the two buildings where Knox-Henderson pricing doesn't sting as much. The median sits at $1,800, so this range covers about half the inventory.

$2,000–$2,300/mo: The full Knox-Henderson market is in play. Rent tops out at $2,217 for 1BR starting prices, so this budget gives you choice across all 21 buildings. At the higher $/sqft end, you're getting a smaller unit in a more premium location.

Budget alternative: If you want the Henderson Avenue lifestyle but not the Henderson Avenue rent, Oak Lawn at $2.05/sqft with 60 buildings is right next door. Same restaurants, same bars — just a short walk or drive instead of your front door.

Getting Around

Henderson Avenue is walkable — that's the whole point. Restaurants, bars, coffee shops, all on foot. The Katy Trail is nearby for running and cycling. For commuting, you're close to Central Expressway (75) and the Knox/Henderson DART station. Uptown is immediately adjacent. Downtown is a 10-minute drive.

Groceries: Central Market on Lovers Lane is the go-to — solid selection, reasonable drive. Trader Joe's on Lower Greenville is close. You're not walking to a grocery store from most buildings, but nothing is far.

Who Does Well Here

People who eat out. A lot. If your social life centers around restaurants and bars, Knox-Henderson puts you at the center of Dallas's best dining strip. Young professionals who value walkable nightlife over square footage. People who've done the Uptown scene and want something a bit more curated.

If your budget is $1,800+ and restaurants matter more than apartment size, this is the neighborhood. If you need space, concessions, or options — Uptown has 115 buildings and runs 9% cheaper per square foot. Oak Lawn has 60 buildings at 33% less.
