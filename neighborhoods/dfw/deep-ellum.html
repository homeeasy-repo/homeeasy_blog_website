The Market

Deep Ellum is the arts-and-nightlife district east of downtown Dallas. 49 buildings in our database, 25 in our portfolio — a solid inventory pool at a good price point. At $2.33/sqft median, it runs 17% cheaper than Uptown ($2.80) and 24% below Knox-Henderson ($3.06). This is the cheapest urban Dallas neighborhood in our data.

With 25 buildings in the pocket, there's real competition for tenants. Entry starts at $1,223, which is about as low as you'll find inside the Dallas core without going suburban.

The trade-off is clear: this is a bar-and-music district first, a residential neighborhood second. Weekends are loud. The streets are built for nightlife, not strollers. If that's your thing, the pricing is hard to beat. If you need quiet, look elsewhere.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Cheapest urban Dallas neighborhood. 25 buildings. Live music on your doorstep. Street art on every block. Walkable bars and restaurants. Entry at $1,223 — accessible for most budgets. DART Green Line access to downtown.

What You Give Up

Loud on weekends, especially Friday and Saturday nights. Not a residential neighborhood in feel. Bar crowds, street noise, and late-night activity are the norm, not the exception. Not for quiet living.

What's Happening in the Market

Deep Ellum has 49 buildings tracked in our database. 25 coverage means there's real selection and competition among the buildings here.

The neighborhood works best for a specific renter: someone who wants nightlife at their front door, doesn't mind noise, and wants to spend less than Uptown or Knox-Henderson for an urban location. That's a real audience, and the buildings know it.

When a good deal opens up at $2.08/sqft in a neighborhood where the median is $2.33, it doesn't sit. If Deep Ellum is on your list, move quickly when something fits.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,300/mo: Marquis on Gaston starts at $1,260 — the lowest entry point in Deep Ellum. At $2.26/sqft, you're getting urban Dallas for less than most suburban alternatives.

$1,300–$1,400/mo: The sweet spot. Marquis at Texas Street ($1,301, $2.08/sqft) and Willow ($1,335, $2.08/sqft) are the best value in the neighborhood — and both are running concessions. This is where most renters land.

$1,400–$1,670/mo: Top of the Deep Ellum range. Larger units, higher floors, more space. At this budget, you could also look at Uptown or Knox-Henderson, but you'll get less square footage per dollar there.

Getting Around

DART Green Line runs through Deep Ellum — one stop to downtown, connections to the rest of the rail network from there. Within the neighborhood, everything is walkable. Bars, restaurants, music venues, coffee shops — all within a few blocks of each other.

A car is still useful for the rest of DFW. Dallas is a driving city. But for day-to-day life inside Deep Ellum, you can get by without one more than most Dallas neighborhoods.

Who Does Well Here

People who want to be in the middle of the nightlife, not adjacent to it. People who work downtown or remote and don't need a quiet suburban street. People who prioritize walkability and culture over space and silence. Young professionals who want urban Dallas at a price point that doesn't require Uptown income.

If you go to bed at 10pm on Fridays, this isn't your neighborhood. If you're the one out until 2am, it might be the best deal in Dallas.
