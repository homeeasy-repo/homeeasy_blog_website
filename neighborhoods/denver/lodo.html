The Market

LoDo -- Lower Downtown -- is where Denver began. The 23-block historic district sits between Union Station and Coors Field, anchored by Larimer Square and the 16th Street Mall. Converted warehouses next to modern towers. Walk Score around 95. Transit Score around 85. One of the few Denver neighborhoods where you do not need a car.

We have 57 buildings in LoDo out of 62 total -- a 92% coverage. Three buildings with rent data on file: TwentyOne01 on Market from $1,405 at $3.11/sqft, Solera from $1,755 at $3.08/sqft, and The Manhattan by Windsor from $1,915 at $3.11/sqft. Entry price is $1,405. Median rent is $1,755. The $/sqft range is tight -- $3.08 to $3.11 -- which means you are paying for square footage here, not brand premium. No active concessions in LoDo right now.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

The best transit hub in Colorado. Converted warehouse character mixed with modern high-rises along the Platte River.

What You Give Up

Getting Around

Walk Score 95. Transit Score 85. Union Station is the hub of RTD's light rail network -- direct lines to DIA, Tech Center, Golden, and the suburbs. Free MallRide shuttle runs the length of 16th Street Mall. Cherry Creek Trail along the Platte River for bike commuting south toward Wash Park or north toward RiNo. Most LoDo residents find a car unnecessary for daily life. This is the most transit-connected neighborhood in Colorado.

Who Does Well Here

Young professionals who work downtown or commute via light rail. People who want to live without a car. Sports fans who want Coors Field walkable. Remote workers who want restaurants and coffee shops within a block. With 57 buildings, there is real selection here -- you are not choosing between two options. If you want cheaper central Denver, RiNo is next door. If you want the most buildings in one neighborhood, Capitol Hill has 118.
