The Market

Cherry Creek is Denver's most expensive neighborhood. 22 total buildings, 19 in our portfolio. Rents run $1,845 to $2,662. Median $/sqft is $2.83, though the range is wide -- High Country House at $2.10/sqft sits at one end, Coda at $4.07/sqft at the other. That spread matters: Cherry Creek is not one price point, it is a neighborhood with options.

The Cherry Creek Shopping Center, the Cherry Creek North boutique corridor, tree-lined residential streets. This is where Denver's retail and dining concentrates. The residential stock is primarily upscale apartments and high-end condos. Zero concessions mapped across all 19 buildings -- no one here is negotiating on price.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

Under $1,900/mo: Gables Vista ($1,845) and Seasons of Cherry Creek ($1,854) are your entry points. Both under $1,900, both in Cherry Creek proper. Gables Vista at $3.10/sqft, Seasons at $2.83/sqft.

$2,000-$2,300/mo: The middle of the market. Coda ($2,075, $4.07/sqft) gives you the highest $/sqft in the neighborhood -- smaller units, premium finishes. High Country House ($2,250, $2.10/sqft) is the opposite play: more space per dollar at Cherry Creek's lowest $/sqft.

$2,500+/mo: Gables Residences ($2,662, $2.74/sqft) is the top of the rent range. Larger floor plans, established property. Plus 14 additional buildings we can match against your specs.

Want Cherry Creek access for less? Capitol Hill (118 buildings) borders Cherry Creek to the north -- direct trail access. Baker (16 buildings) and Wash Park (8 buildings) are adjacent to the south. All give you Cherry Creek proximity without the Cherry Creek price floor.

Getting Around

Walk Score around 80 in the commercial core. Cherry Creek North is walkable for shopping, dining, and services. Cherry Creek Trail provides a car-free commute route to downtown via bike -- the trail runs directly through the neighborhood. RTD bus routes connect to downtown and other neighborhoods. University and Colorado Boulevards are major arterials. Not as transit-rich as Capitol Hill, but the trail system and walkable core make it more car-optional than most Denver neighborhoods.

Who Does Well Here

Professionals who want Denver's retail and dining core within walking distance. Couples and empty nesters who prioritize polished surroundings over nightlife. Budget-conscious renters who know to look at High Country House ($2.10/sqft) instead of assuming Cherry Creek means $4/sqft across the board. The range here is real -- $2.10 to $4.07/sqft -- and that spread creates options most people do not expect in Denver's most expensive neighborhood.
