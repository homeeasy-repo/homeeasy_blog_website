The Market

Baker is a character neighborhood. South Broadway is the spine -- antique shops, dive bars, record stores, taquerias, independently owned storefronts that have been here longer than the new construction around them. The Art District on Santa Fe borders Baker to the west with First Friday gallery walks. This is not a polished downtown corridor. It has personality.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

South Broadway character -- vintage shops, Antique Row, taquerias, dive bars, live music. Art District on Santa Fe with First Friday gallery walks. Front Range mountain views from many buildings. South Platte River Trail for bike commuting. Walk Score around 80. Entry from $1,599/mo. Light rail at I-25/Broadway station. 16 buildings to compare against each other.

What You Give Up

Zero active concessions. Smaller selection than Capitol Hill (118 buildings) or the broader metro. Not a high-rise neighborhood -- expect mid-rise and walk-up construction. South Broadway nightlife means noise on weekend evenings along the commercial corridor. Parking can be competitive near Broadway.

Getting Around

Walk Score around 80. Broadway provides a dense commercial corridor within walking distance. RTD bus routes on Broadway and South Santa Fe Drive connect to downtown in about 10 minutes. South Platte River Trail runs along the western edge for car-free cycling to downtown and Confluence Park. Light rail at I-25/Broadway station connects to the Tech Center and south suburbs. Bikeable and relatively walkable for Denver.

Who Does Well Here

Renters who want neighborhood character over downtown polish. People who prefer independently owned restaurants and dive bars over chain retail. Artists and creatives drawn to the Santa Fe Art District. Anyone who wants central Denver without paying Cherry Creek prices.
