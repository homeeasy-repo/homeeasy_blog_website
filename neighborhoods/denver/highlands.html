The Market

The Highlands sits across the Platte River from downtown Denver. 32nd Avenue is one of the city's best restaurant corridors. Tennyson Street in West Highland has coffee shops, bookstores, and wine bars. The adjacent LoHi (Lower Highlands) sub-neighborhood offers skyline and Front Range mountain views from its bluff. Sloan's Lake provides outdoor access to the west. Highland Bridge connects directly to LoDo -- a 10-minute walk to downtown.

The neighborhood has a family-friendly character with an urban feel. Tree-lined streets of vintage bungalows and Victorians sit alongside newer mid-rise apartment buildings. Farmers markets and community events run through the warmer months. The restaurant and bar density is high enough that most residents do not need to leave the neighborhood for dining.

Getting Around

Walk Score around 80. 32nd Avenue, Tennyson Street, and the LoHi restaurant strip provide walkable commercial corridors. Highland Bridge connects to LoDo and downtown -- a 10-minute walk across the Platte River. RTD bus routes serve the main corridors. The 38th and Blake light rail station in nearby RiNo provides rail access to DIA and the southern suburbs. Platte River Trail is accessible from the eastern edge for bike commuting downtown.

Who Does Well Here

People who want a neighborhood that functions as its own small town -- restaurants, bars, coffee, groceries all within walking distance. Families who want tree-lined streets without leaving the city.
