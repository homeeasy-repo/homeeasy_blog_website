The Market

$1,115 at Edit at River North. That is the cheapest entry into central Denver in our portfolio. Lower than Capitol Hill, lower than LoDo, lower than Highlands. If your budget is tight but you want brewery-district energy and a 15-minute walk to downtown, this is where the numbers work.

RiNo is Denver's industrial-to-creative conversion story. Former warehouses turned into brewery taprooms, street art on every loading dock, food halls like The Source and Zeppelin Station anchoring the district. The apartment stock follows the same pattern -- converted industrial and new construction built around it. We have 37 buildings out of 45 total in RiNo, with 2 running active concessions. Across the Denver metro, our portfolio covers 694 buildings out of 865 total.

The Trade-Offs

What You Get

Cheapest central Denver entry at $1,115. 37 buildings -- real selection, not a token count. 2 active concessions (Forge and The Riv). Denver's highest concentration of craft breweries. The Source and Zeppelin Station for dining. 38th and Blake light rail station. Cherry Creek Trail access. 15-20 minute walk to LoDo and downtown.

What You Give Up

Parts of RiNo are still industrial, not residential. Some blocks east of Brighton Boulevard are rougher after dark. The art-district energy also means weekend noise from taprooms and events. If you need walkability scores above 85, Capitol Hill (Walk Score 90) is a better fit -- but you will pay $1,813+ to start there.

What Your Budget Gets You Here

$1,100-$1,300/mo: Edit at River North at $1,115 entry, $2.71/sqft. The cheapest way into central Denver in our portfolio. Studio or small 1-bedroom. Modern construction in the art district.

$1,400-$1,600/mo: Forge ($1,450, $3.05/sqft, has concession) or Edison at RiNo ($1,513, $3.09/sqft). More space, higher finishes. Forge is running a concession -- ask us for current terms.

$1,900+/mo: The Riv at $1,906 entry, $3.64/sqft. Top-tier finishes. Also running a concession. The premium end of RiNo.

Denver context: For comparison, Capitol Hill starts at $1,813 (118 buildings), LoDo at $1,405 (57 buildings). RiNo gives you central Denver at the lowest entry price with 37 buildings to choose from.

Getting Around

Walk Score around 75. 38th and Blake light rail station connects to downtown, DIA, and the suburbs. Brighton Boulevard has protected bike lanes. Cherry Creek Trail runs along the western edge for car-free commuting. Walking to LoDo takes 15-20 minutes. Flat and bikeable -- many residents bike as primary transportation. B-cycle stations throughout the district.

Who Does Well Here

Budget-conscious renters who want central Denver without central Denver prices. Creatives and beer enthusiasts. People who prefer street art over manicured landscaping. If you need the cheapest rent in central Denver, Edit at River North at $1,115 is the answer. If you want concession deals, Forge and The Riv are both running them. If you want deeper selection in one neighborhood, Capitol Hill has 118 buildings but starts $700/mo higher.
